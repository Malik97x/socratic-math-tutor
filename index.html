Problem 1: The High Five Problem

Problem Statement

There are 26 people in a room. If every person gives every other person exactly one high five, how many high fives happen in total?

Goal Question

Figure out a formula that works for ANY number of people (n).

Archiving work completes the task.

Problem 2: The Phone Plan Problem

Problem Statement

Phone Plan A: $30 per month + $0.10 per minute.
Phone Plan B: $50 per month + $0.05 per minute.

Goal Questions

  • How many minutes make Plan B cheaper than Plan A?
  • Can you find a rule that works for ANY two phone plans?
Think about what 'per minute' means in an equation. Set the two costs equal to each other!

Problem 4: The Staircase Problem

Problem Statement

A staircase has 1 block in the 1st step, 2 in the 2nd, 3 in the 3rd, and so on.

  • Part A: Total blocks in a staircase with 10 steps?
  • Part B: Formula for total blocks with n steps.
  • Part C: How many steps can you build with 1,000 blocks?
Look for the Fibonacci pattern.
